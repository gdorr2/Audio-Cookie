What Is Podcasting?

Sharing information through spoken word played a significant role in developing human civilization. The means through which we have grown our ability to connect, share and spread news has led to our world today. What are podcasts, and how do they work? Podcasts are prepared audio files using RSS feeds to subscribers' computers or mobile devices. Podcasts can be found on a variety of platforms like Spotify. Apple Podcasts, SoundCloud, and more. The term podcast is a combination of the words iPod and broadcast. Writer Ben Hammersly first used the term in an article he had written for the Guardian in 2004. The first podcast was created in 2004 by Adam Curry, a former MTV video jockey, and Dave Winer, a software engineer.

Podcasts can be about anything and are in an accessible format. Some podcasts are like audio stories, and some are formatted around having guest interviews like late-night talk shows, which is a strength of the format. There are very few upstart costs to do your podcast. A podcast could be you and a friend recording a conversation using your phone and then uploading the file to youtube for audiences is an example of a low-entry podcast. The freedom of podcasting has allowed for more representation and diversity in our society and culture. Podcasts have grown in popularity to the point where the top performing podcast can make earnings and have started media companies. One such company is Barstool Sports. Barstool has many podcasts on sports and other topics and has developed into a multimillion-dollar media company in nearly 20 years.
